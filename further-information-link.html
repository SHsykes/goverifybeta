The collection and storage of your personal documents and information (Privacy Notice)

We will hold your data in accordance with GDPR legislation.

What is the legal basis for collecting this data?

GDPR legislation requires a lawful basis for processing personal data. We hold your data to perform tasks carried out in the public interest, with your consent. These tasks are the administration of council services which you have applied for.

The categories of information that we process include:

  • personal identifiers and contacts (such as name, contact details and address)
  • proof of address, including but not limited to: council tax bills or utility bills.
  • proof of identity, including but not limited to: passports, driving licences, birth certificates
  • proof of benefits such as benefits award letters.
How will we use the data transferred to the council?

We will use the documents you provide to support applications you make to our services.

Giving your consent

We will ask you to give your consent to the following actions:

  • storage of your documents on the secure council web servers for a period of 5 years.
  • access to your documents by authorised members of council staff.
Withdrawing your consent

You can withdraw your consent to the storage of your documents at any time. To withdraw your consent, please email admin@fictionalcitycouncil.gov.uk. We will remove your documents from the system within 24 hours of receipt of your email.

Duration of storage

We will not store your documents for any longer than needed. The duration of storage will depend on the service you have applied for. Some services need you to renew your application every year. We will ask you whether you wish to store your documents in the Portal for a longer period of time to cover renewals.

What we do with your data

Council staff will have access to your data to process any applications you make.

We will not:

  • sell or rent your data to third parties
  • share your data with third parties for marketing purposes

We will share your data if we’re required to do so by law – for example, by court order, or to prevent fraud or other crime.

We will share your email address with relevant staff if you opt in to our email notification service.

Who is responsible for your data?

Fictional City Council, as a data controller, are responsible for your data.
